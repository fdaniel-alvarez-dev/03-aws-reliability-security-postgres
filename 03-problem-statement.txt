03 - Problem Statement (Repository Validation Contract)

Purpose
This document defines the business context, technical requirements, and evaluation criteria that this repository is expected to satisfy.
It is intentionally company- and role-agnostic.

Business context (generic)
- A team operates business-critical relational databases and must ship changes with minimal downtime and risk.
- The environment is cloud-hosted and cost-sensitive.
- Reliability and security must be demonstrable with runbooks, drills, and tests.

Technical requirements (what must be true)
1) Postgres reliability foundations
   - A realistic HA lab (primary + replica) and connection pooling.
   - Replication verification with clear, actionable output.
   - Backup + restore workflows that produce artifacts and can be repeated.

2) Migration readiness
   - Documentation and operational patterns for safe rollout strategies.
   - Automation-friendly workflows that reduce manual steps and ambiguity.

3) Security and operability
   - No secrets committed.
   - CI includes secret scanning and basic quality gates.
   - Tests have two explicit modes:
     - demo: mocks/fakes only (no external calls)
     - production: real integrations when correctly configured and explicitly acknowledged

Top 3 pain points this repository must solve
1) “Backups exist, but restores are unpracticed and unreliable.”
2) “High availability and replication behavior are not verified.”
3) “Migrations are risky because procedures are undocumented and untested.”
